Detailed Clinical Information

Understanding the AI classifications and general treatment guidelines for detected anomalies.

Glioma

Gliomas are a common type of tumor originating in the brain or spinal cord. They begin in the gluey supportive cells (glial cells) that surround nerve cells and help them function.

Standard Treatment Approach:

  • Surgical removal when possible (resection).
  • Radiation therapy targeting the tumor bed.
  • Chemotherapy (e.g., Temozolomide).
  • Targeted drug therapy based on genetic profile.

Meningioma

A meningioma is a tumor that arises from the meninges — the membranes that surround your brain and spinal cord. Most are noncancerous (benign), though rarely they can be malignant.

Standard Treatment Approach:

  • Active observation closely monitoring slow-growing tumors.
  • Surgical excision for symptomatic or growing tumors.
  • Radiosurgery for inaccessible or residual tumor fragments.

Pituitary Tumor

These are abnormal growths that develop in your pituitary gland. Some pituitary tumors result in too many of the hormones that regulate important functions of your body.

Standard Treatment Approach:

  • Transsphenoidal surgery to remove the growth.
  • Medication to block excess hormone secretion.
  • Radiation therapy if surgery isn't viable.

Medical Disclaimer: The classifications provided by NeuroPredict are AI-assisted probabilities and are explicitly designed to aid licensed medical professionals. They do not constitute a definitive medical diagnosis. Always correlate AI findings with comprehensive clinical evaluation and biopsy results before initiating treatment plans.
